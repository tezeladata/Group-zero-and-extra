First Crusade

Historical event

The First Crusade was the first of a series of religious wars, or Crusades,
initiated, supported and at times directed by the Latin Church in the medieval period.
The objective was the recovery of the Holy Land from Islamic rule.

The First Crusade began in 1096 and concluded in 1099.
It was initiated in response to the Byzantine Emperor
Alexios I Komnenos' call for military aid against the Seljuk Turks
and the desire to recapture Jerusalem from Muslim control.
The main military campaigns occurred between 1096 and 1099,
culminating in the capture of Jerusalem by the Crusaders on July 15, 1099.
